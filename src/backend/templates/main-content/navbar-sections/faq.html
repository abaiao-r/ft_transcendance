Pong is a classic arcade game that simulates table tennis. Players control paddles on each side of the screen to hit a ball back and forth, with the objective of scoring points by getting the ball past the opponent's paddle.
Pong was created by Atari, an American video game company founded by Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney. The game was released in 1972 and became one of the first commercially successful video games.
To play Pong, use the paddle to hit the ball back and forth with your opponent. Move the paddle up and down to control its position and angle. The player who misses the ball loses a point, and the first player to reach 10 points wins the game.
In addition to the classic 1v1 mode, you can play Double Pong, 4 players (1 vs 1 vs 1 vs 1), and Pong Tournaments.
