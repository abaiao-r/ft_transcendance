History

Pong, the game that launched a thousand joysticks, burst onto the scene in 1972, captivating players with its addictive simplicity. With just two paddles and a bouncing ball, it sparked a gaming revolution.

Atari's daring move to release a home edition in 1975 saw 150,000 units fly off shelves, cementing Pong's status as a cultural icon. Despite facing fierce competition from clones, Atari's innovative spirit shone with creations like "Double Pong," proving that in the game of gaming, innovation is king.

Pong's legacy lives on, a timeless reminder that big revolutions can start with just a small bounce.
